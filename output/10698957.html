Health Vacancy N.N. 10698957
Australian Commission on Safety and Quality in Health Care (ACSQHC) Closing date: Monday, 30 January 2017
Job Title: Senior Project Officer
Division: National Standards
Job Type: Ongoing, Full-time
Salary: $97,546 - $111,253
Location: Sydney - NSW
Classification: Executive Level 1
Agency Employment Act: PS Act 1999
Position No: Several
Agency Website: http://www.safetyandquality.gov.au/

Job Description

Duties


This position is located in the National Standards Program. The Program’s purpose is to develop and maintain the National Safety and Quality Health Service (NSQHS) Standards and resources, and support implementation of the Standards and the Australian Health Services Safety and Quality Accreditation (AHSSQA) Scheme.

The Senior Project Officer will be responsible for managing the day to day work of program projects and will be expected to work with limited supervision, within a team context and with internal and external stakeholders. The position requires excellent skills in communication, problem solving, time management and research and would suit a high performing candidate.

Position duties and responsibilities
1. Undertake project management activities related to the NSQHS Standards and AHSSQA Scheme including:
• project planning and documentation
• implementation and monitoring of project milestones
• presenting and / or report on progress
• identification and management of risks.
2. Review and analyse research, data, reports, websites and other published material relevant to the program area.
3. Consult with internal and external stakeholders to facilitate the development of specific projects relevant to the program area.
4. Prepare written reports, briefs, agendas, papers, presentations and other documents for a range of audiences.
5. Establish and facilitate the project’s working groups/ committees/ forums/ workshops/consultative process and represent the Commission at relevant meetings and provide secretariat support to the work of relevant committees and expert groups.
6. Inform the Program Director, Program team and Commission Executive and other relevant stakeholders about the progress and strategic implications of projects.
7. Participate on the roster of the Commission Advice Centre available to health services seeking practical and technical advice when implementing the National Safety and Quality Health Service (NSQHS) Standards.

 

Eligibility


• Qualification as a health professional with clinical experience.
• Tertiary qualifications in a relevant area of health.
• Project management and/or policy development experience at a state or national level.
