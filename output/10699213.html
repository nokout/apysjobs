Job Description

Duties


OUR OPPORTUNITIES

The Crisis Coordination Centre (CCC) is an all-hazards, 24/7 coordination facility, which monitors and informs the Australian Government of emerging threats during a crisis. The CCC plans for and coordinates information flow and the Australian Government's responses to national security and emergency management events, both in Australia and internationally.

The CCC has a key role in supporting the Australian Government Crisis Committee (AGCC) and the National Crisis Committee (NCC) in monitoring, informing, planning and coordinating crises both domestically and, in support of the Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade, internationally.

The purpose of the CCC is to centralise an Australian Government response to a crisis by providing a nation-wide all-hazards coordination and information sharing network. This includes Australian Government and state and territory agencies. The CCC provides whole-of-government all-hazards monitoring and situational awareness for domestic and international events.

There are up to three APS 5 Senior Information Officer positions are available.

Please note: these positions are a designated shift work role and the occupants are required to perform duties on a 24/7 rotational roster. Full time shift workers receive an annualised penalty payment and additional leave on top of their full time salaries.

OUR SUCCESSFUL APPLICANTS

• Provide ongoing all-hazards situational awareness to CCC stakeholders.

• Monitor incoming agency feeds, open source media and the CCCs incident and information systems and oversee the development of CCC briefing and analytical products for senior executive decision makers, Australian Government and jurisdictional agencies, as per standard operating procedures.

• Interpret, evaluate and produce CCC briefing and analytical products for senior executive decision makers and Australian Government and jurisdictional agencies.

• Participate in Incident Management Teams when required.

• Assist the Assistant Directors and Team Leaders in ensuring ongoing development and maintenance of standard operating procedures, guidelines and other tools to ensure consistency and efficiency within the CCC.

• Assist the Team Leader with stakeholder liaison and maintain working relationships with key stakeholders (internal/external), state and territory governments and Commonwealth and International Partners, ensuring CCC Products are meeting stakeholder requirements.

• Assist the Team Leader in guiding the work for CCC Information Officers.

• Perform the National Security Hotline supervisory role when required.

• Perform the CCC Information Officer role when required.

• Participate in and initiate learning and development activities, as required. Perform other duties as directed.

• Act as Team Leader as required.
 

Eligibility


• Applicants must be Australian citizens to be eligible for employment at the Attorney-General’s Department.

• The preferred applicant will be required to successfully undergo a police record check and be able to obtain and maintain a Negative Vetting 1 security clearance level. The preferred applicant must be willing to disclose all relevant and required information.

• Successful candidates will need to satisfy AFP Building access requirements and have the ability to work shift work.

About Attorney-General's Department 

The Attorney-General's Department (AGD) serves the people of Australia by delivering programs and policies to maintain and improve Australia’s law and justice framework, and strengthen our national security and emergency management. Through the Australian Government Solicitor, we also provide legal services to the Commonwealth (including legal advice and representation).

We are the central policy and coordinating element of the Attorney-General’s portfolio, for which the Attorney-General; the Minister for Justice and Minister Assisting the Prime Minister for Counter-Terrorism are responsible.

To find out more, visit our website at www.ag.gov.au.
