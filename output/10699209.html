Communications and the Arts Vacancy N.N. 10699209
Australian Communications and Media Authority Closing date: Sunday, 22 January 2017

Division: Office of the Children's eSafety Commissioner
Branch: Executive Manager
Section: Cybersmart Operations
Job Type: Ongoing, Non-ongoing (Temporary), Full-time
Salary: $93,353 - $105,318
Location: Sydney - NSW
Classification: Executive Level 1
Agency Employment Act: PS Act 1999
Position No: 1747

Job Description

Duties


The Office of the Children's eSafety Commissioner (the Office) is committed to helping young people have safe, positive experiences online and encouraging behavioural change, where a generation of Australian children act responsibly online - just as they would offline. We provide online safety education for Australian children and young people, a complaints service for young Australians who experience serious cyberbullying, and address illegal online content through the Online Content Scheme. Our goal is to empower all Australians to explore the online world-safely.

Recently, the Office assumed responsibility for the establishment of a national online complaints mechanism, to assist Australians to access tangible support when intimate images or videos are posted without their consent.

The Senior Communications Advisor plays a critical role in the development and implementation of the Office's communications and marketing plan to support the delivery of the Office’s objectives.

This vacancy is available on an ongoing basis.
 

Eligibility


To be eligible for employment with the ACMA applicants must be an Australian citizen. An applicant's suitability for employment with the ACMA will be assessed through a pre-employment screening process. This process will include a requirement for the applicant to undergo and satisfy an AFP National Police Check and to complete a Health Declaration.

 

Notes


Non-ongoing opportunity will be offered for a specified term

An order of merit may be established and used to fill similar ongoing or non-ongoing vacancies at this level. Non-ongoing vacancies filled from an order of merit may be offered as a specified term up to 18 months.

About Australian Communications and Media Authority 

ACMA is Australia’s regulator for broadcasting, the internet, radiocommunications and telecommunications.

We work with industry and government, locally and internationally, so that Australians can enjoy the best and most innovative media and communications services in a fair, responsible, safe and productive way.

The ACMA provides a supportive and respectful work environment that values the diversity of our employees. Our three main offices are in Canberra, Melbourne and Sydney. Visit acma.gov.au for more information on joining our team
