Health Vacancy N.N. 10698692
Australian Commission on Safety and Quality in Health Care (ACSQHC) Closing date: Sunday, 15 January 2017
Job Title: Program Manager - Patient Safety Measurement
Division: Safety and Quality Improvement Systems and Intergovermental Relations
Job Type: Ongoing, Full-time
Salary: $116,382 - $137,790
Location: Sydney - NSW
Classification: Executive Level 2
Agency Employment Act: PS Act 1999
Position No: 20021622
Agency Website: http://www.safetyandquality.gov.au/

Job Description

Duties


This position will lead a small team to manage the delivery of the Safety & Quality Improvement Systems & Intergovernmental Relations team’s commitments to:
• develop strategies, standards and tools to support the measurement, monitoring and reporting of safety and quality in health care
• incorporate safety and quality into pricing of public hospital services in Australia – for example, collaboration with the Independent Hospital Pricing Authority (IHPA).

Position duties and responsibilities
1. Coordinate the Commission’s development of a multi-faceted patient safety measurement model, within a strategic framework developed by the Commission in consultation with jurisdictions, national agencies, and private and primary health care providers.

2. Work collaboratively with national agencies, expert groups, jurisdictions and the private hospital sector in the development of national arrangements for clinical quality registries.

3. Work collaboratively with national agencies, expert groups, jurisdictions and the private hospital sector on the curation of the Hospital acquired complications (HACs) list and development of supporting resources for the HACs list that aim to improve clinical documentation and support local monitoring.

4. Establish and manage project reference groups, and undertake project management activities including:
a. Planning and documenting projects;
b. Managing project budgets;
c. Implementing, monitoring and reporting on progress;
d. Providing recommendations about managing risk, identifying problems and proposing possible solutions;
e. Reporting on project progress and outcomes; and
f. Managing project deliverables to completion.

5. Work through relevant Australian Health Ministers Advisory Committee (AHMAC) principal and sub-committees in the preparation and presentation of Commission papers.

6. Work effectively with the Safety and Quality Improvement Systems and Intergovernmental Relations team, other Commission program areas and national expert groups and agencies.

7. Support the Commission’s clinical quality registries, HACs and patient safety measurement project streams, including monitoring and reporting on progress of the program work streams, managing risks, and identifying and resolving issues.

8. Provide secretariat support and participate in the work of expert working groups or workshops convened for the development of national arrangements for clinical quality registries, patient safety measurement and HACs, including the preparation of background papers, presentation and briefing papers.

9. Manage, as required, the commissioning of literature reviews, research and consultancies in support of these programs.

10. Participate, as required, in related health information initiatives within the Safety and Quality Improvement Systems and Intergovernmental Relations work program.

11. Prepare briefings and background papers on specific issues and project progress.

 

Eligibility


• Tertiary qualification in relevant area.
• Understanding of and experience in the Australian health system.
• Experience in management of healthcare programs, health services research, and data-driven quality improvement projects.
• Experience in liaising and consulting with clinical expert and health information groups to achieve specific outputs and agreements.

 

Notes
