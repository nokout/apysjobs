Attorney-General Vacancy N.N. 10699065
Australian Financial Security Authority Closing date: Sunday, 22 January 2017
Job Title: ICT Disaster Recovery Manager
Division: ICT Services
Job Type: Ongoing, Full-time
Salary: $95,688 - $112,640
Location: Canberra - ACT
Classification: Executive Level 1
Agency Employment Act: PS Act 1999
Position No: 30406 | NP
Agency Website: http://www.afsa.gov.au

Job Description

Duties


**********************************************************************************************************************************
AFSA actively promotes flexibility with working options in this role.
We encourage flexible work patterns, flex time to support your family commitments and personal interests.
**********************************************************************************************************************************

The ICT Disaster Recovery Manager will be responsible for ensuring AFSA’s ICT disaster recovery plans are tested, evaluated and aligned to AFSA’s Business Continuity Framework (BCF) and Business Continuity Plan (BCP). This includes an ability to extract both business and technical requirements from a varied audience and disseminate them into DR technical requirements and solutions.
The DR Manager will work closely with all sections within the ICT Division, AFSA’s Business Continuity Teams and key internal and external stakeholders.

Key responsibilities include:

Analysis and Requirements Management
• Work with AFSA’s Business Continuity Teams and key stakeholders to understand systems recovery requirements.
• Analyse ICT Division operational performance data to identify issues or trends that may impact continuity of AFSA’s ICT services and require attention.
• Monitor changes in business process and new services and assess changes to ICT service continuity requirements.
• Monitor technical changes for impact to continuity capabilities.
• Maintain continuity requirements documentation and communicate new requirements to relevant staff.

Strategy Development and Solution Coordination
• Lead development and delivery of the ICT DR strategy and roadmap, in consultation with key stakeholders.
• Liaise with architecture and design functions in the production/update of designs and design documentation.
• Liaise with infrastructure teams for the development and deployment of solutions.

Maintaining Preparedness
• Develop and maintain disaster recovery and continuity related plans and relevant artefacts, such as Standard Operating Procedures and Work Instructions.
• Develop and maintain the testing schedule, in line with AFSA’s Business Continuity Test Strategy and Program.
• Coordinate ICT testing activities including plan walkthroughs and technical testing.
• Escalate risks and issues to the Director, ICT Governance as appropriate.

Crisis Management Support
• Support the CIO, Director, ICT Governance and other senior ICT and AFSA staff in the facilitation of ICT crisis management activities.
• Coordinate crisis management testing activities such as call-tree tests and crisis simulations.
• Maintain an action log during crisis

Develop and maintain ICT DR education and awareness materials, coordinate awareness raising activities and other required communication activities to ensure ICT staff understand DR issues and responsibilities.

Contribute to a high performing ICT Governance section, and more broadly the ICT Client Services Division, by:
o encouraging and supporting of ongoing development opportunities,
o providing regular feedback to colleagues and managers, and
o identifying opportunities to enhance team performance in an environment of collaboration, continuous improvement and change

AFSA’s work environment

You will enjoy excellent conditions including generous superannuation (15.4%), a modern work environment, flexibility to balance work and personal life, and professional growth.
We encourage applications from Indigenous Australians, peoples from culturally diverse backgrounds and people with disabilities. We value employee commitment and contribution and we provide a working environment that supports staff to reach their full potential.

 

Eligibility


Please note that this opportunity is open only to Australian Citizens.

The successful applicant must have, or be willing to undergo a security clearance to the level of Baseline vetting as a condition of employment.

To satisfy character requirements all AFSA employees must undergo a police records check.

Where a person has received a redundancy benefit from APS agency employment and their corresponding redundancy benefit period has not expired, they may be ineligible for employment.
 

Notes


Email applications should clearly state your name, the position title and location in the subject line.

Your application will be assessed against work related qualities and essential capabilities of the position and must include:
• A completed application cover sheet
• A current resume
• A covering letter (2 pages max) stating why you are interested in the position, how your skills and experience make you the best person for the position and what value you can add to AFSA and the ICT Services division

Candidates may be required to undergo psychometric and/or work sample testing as part of this selection process.
