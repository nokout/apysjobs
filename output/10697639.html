Statutory Authorities Vacancy N.N. 10697639
Australian Federal Police Closing date: Thursday, 23 March 2017
Job Title: Hardware Engineers and Software Developers
Division: Covert & Capability
Branch: Digital Surveillance Collection Team
Job Type: Ongoing, Full-time
Salary: $90,517 - $116,428
Location: Barton - ACT
Agency Classification: AFP Band 6 - AFP Band 8
Position No: ROI 0618/16

Job Description

Duties


The Australian Federal Police (AFP) is establishing a Register of Interest (ROI) of appropriately skilled and experienced individuals for a range of technical specialist roles for Hardware Engineers and Software Developers in its Digital Surveillance Collection Team, AFP Bands 6-8. When applying for the ROI we invite you to submit a maximum of 300 words outlining the skills you would bring to the role. Please indicate the area of work you have expertise in, the areas you are interested in and also your educational qualifications, experience and skills. A current resume also needs to be attached. Applicants identified with the appropriate skills will be invited to apply for positions when they are advertised during March – April 2017. If you have already submitted an application for VRN 0520/16 AFP Band 6-8 Hardware Engineers and Software Developers there is no requirement to register your interest.

The AFP operates in a global technologically enriched environment, and in doing so recognises the reality that criminals, including terrorist groups, are increasingly utilising sophisticated technology to facilitate their illegal activities across multi jurisdictions. As a response, the AFP has focussed one of its strategic initiatives in its AFP’s Corporate Plan 2016-17, to invest in an agile and innovative capability, including the recruitment of skilled staff.

The AFP’s Digital Surveillance Collection (DSC) unit provides technical support and capability to AFP operations through the development and delivery of digital surveillance solutions. DSC’s mix of highly skilled software, hardware, data, communication and project management specialists provides a unique capability in terms of addressing the increasing challenges technology presents to law enforcement activities.

DSC’s software and hardware engineering and development services, combined with access to trusted industry and collaboration with partner agencies, provides agile yet world leading solutions to covert and discreet digital surveillance. DSC is currently recruiting experienced hardware and software engineers and developers for current and future vacancies in our Canberra office. These positions are across the Band 6 to Band 8 roles as prescribed under the current AFP Enterprise Agreement 2012-2016.

These roles require an ability to meet tight deadlines, prioritise conflicting tasks and deliver technical solutions for DSC’s support to operations. DSC emphasises collaboration within and between teams, external agencies and trusted industry partners as being key to delivering effective and sustainable solutions to complex technical challenges. High level communication skills are essential including an ability to communicate complex technical concepts to both technical and non-technical stakeholders.

The work may include:

• Applying high level technical engineering and development skills and experience to access new and emerging technology
• Low level engineering and development
• Collaboration with industry and other agencies to develop bespoke technical solutions to policing challenges
• Undertaking and overseeing project or research work to support AFP business
• Management of short and long term technical development and maintenance projects
• Responding to operational technical demands including outside of standard hours and in support of field work
• Mentoring more junior team members in engineering and development processes and technology
• Maintaining currency in subject matter expertise through ongoing professional development and learning opportunities outside the formal education process
• International travel to co-develop, collaborate and conduct research.

Contact Officer: technical.recruitment@afp.gov.au or (02) 6131 3535
Closing Date: 4pm (AEDT) Thursday 23rd March 2017

About Australian Federal Police 

The mission of the AFP is to provide dynamic and effective law enforcement to the people of Australia. It provides policing throughout Australia in relation to the prevention and detection of crimes against the Commonwealth, its laws and integrity, and community police services to the Community of the ACT.

The AFP is committed to recruiting from the diverse groups that make up Australian society. We believe that all Australians should have the opportunity to compete for careers in the AFP without being disadvantaged by gender, race, ethnicity, disability, sexual orientation, age, attitude or other difference.
