Job Description

Duties


The Support Coordinator is responsible for developing, implementing, monitoring and refining training programs, materials and procedures for external users and team members, coordinating the implementation of new and modified systems across the blood-sector, and coordinating the activities of APS4 Support Officers to achieve desired outcomes and operational objectives.

Key Duties

1. Be the primary contact within the Blood Operations Centre (BOC), responsible for answering, analysing and responding to level one and two issues raised by internal and external clients within agreed timeframes to the satisfaction of the client;
2. Draft standard operating procedures, correspondence, training plans, newsletters and educational training materials;
3. Perform all aspects of project and change coordination, including but not limited to: project planning, monitoring, implementation, resource management, communications, stakeholder engagement, project reporting and risk management;
4. Work collaboratively with a diverse range of stakeholders at all levels within the blood sector;
5. Perform secretariat duties for a variety of User Groups, including coordinating logistics and travel arrangements, collating meeting papers, preparing minutes and taking action to implement and communicate committee decisions;
6. Represent the NBA at industry conferences and participate in meetings with external stakeholders and contracted service providers (as required); and
7. Lead and mentor BOC Support Officer team members.

Eligibility


1. Australian citizenship;
2. The ability to obtain and maintain a security clearance at 'Negative Vetting Level 1';
3. Recent experience in a laboratory or hospital environment (highly desirable); and
4. Recent experience or qualifications in Project Management/Coordination and/or training (highly desirable).



Notes


1. Candidates are required to complete an NBA Application Cover Sheet as part of the process. NBA Application Cover Sheets can be found at https://www.blood.gov.au/employment;
2. The hours of work are normally set through a roster within the hours of 07:00 to 19:00 Monday to Friday. The Support Coordinator may be required to work some weekends and public holidays to provide support to end users;
3.Requirement to undertake on-call duties (including the provision of telephone and email support) outside normal working hours on a rostered basis to support end users;
4. Undertake interstate travel to undertake their duties (as required); and
5. Required to wear NBA uniform when representing the NBA.

About National Blood Authority

The NBA is a small portfolio agency of the Department of Health with an employee base of approximately 55 employees. We are based in Lyneham in the ACT and as a high performing, award winning statutory authority, the NBA is focused on “Saving and improving Australian lives through a world-class blood supply”. With this vision at the heart of our NBA community, we strive for high achievement whilst enjoying a strong team spirit and respecting healthy work life balances. We are committed to excellence in servicing the Australian blood sector and work cooperatively with our stakeholders which encompasses all governments (state-based and Commonwealth), clinicians, the health sector and the Australian community.
